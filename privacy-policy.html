Use of Cookies

A cookie is a very small text document, which often includes an anonymous unique identifier. When you visit an app, that site's computer or mobile phone asks your computer or mobile phone for permission to store file. Each app can send its own cookie to your browser if your browser's preferences allow it, but (to protect your privacy) your browser only permits a app to access the cookies it has already sent to you, not the cookies sent to you by other sites.

IP Addresses

IP addresses are used by your computer or mobile phone every time you are connected to the Internet. Your IP address is a number that is used by computer or mobile phones on the network to identify your computer or mobile phone. IP addresses are automatically collected by our web server as part of demographic and profile data known as “traffic data” so that data (such as the Web pages you request) can be sent to you.
